Nouns


What are nouns?

Nouns are words that name something such as person, place, thing or idea.

Names Places Things
Albert Einstein Mount Vesuvius Shoe
the president Disneyland basketball
my mother my bedroom freedom
a girl

Proper nouns vs Common nouns

Proper nouns:

Specific name of a person, place or thing.(Always capitalized)

Does Tina have much homework to do this evening?

I would like to visit Canada.


Common nouns:

Generic name of an item in a class or group. (Not capitalized, only if it is at the beginning of a sentence)

The girl crossed the river.


Types of common nouns


Concrete:

Something real.

Plural nouns

Adding -s or -es at to the singular form makes a noun plural. (there are many exeptions)

  • cat - cats: These two cats are both black.
  • tax - taxes
  • house - houses

Possesive nouns

Most of the nouns show the possesive with an apostrophe and an s. ('s)

  • The cat's toy was missing.
  • I have been invited to the boss's house for dinner.
  • Mrs. Sanchez's coat is still hanging on the back of her chair.

Plural nouns ending in s take only an apostrophe to form a possesive.

  • My niece's prom dresses were exquisite.
